Chenyu Zhang

I am an undergraduate student at Johns Hopkins University. I was born in Nanjing, China, and I currently live in Baltimore, Maryland, USA. This portfolio webpage is written during my internship at Google in summer 2020.

At JHU, my majors are Applied Math, Computer Science, and Cognitive Science. I enjoy all three of my majors and I think they complement each other very well. Aside from school, I am part of the Blue Orchid Dance Club, where we practice trational Chineses dances and give performances at various events (which are, sadly, suspended due to COVID-19). I am also a research assistant at the Mind Brain Institute, and a software developer on the Otoglobal Health design team.

My Projects & Roles

Spam Slayer Web App, Sep 2019

I made Spam Slayer with a couple of friends at HopHacks. This web application is a Amazon review filter that, given a product URL, detects fake reviews using an SCNN algorithm and displays genuine reviews. It is not yet deployed, but you can check out how it works at this YouTube link.

Intern at Megapro Corporation, May 2019 - Aug 2019

During freshman summer, I interned at Megapro, a trading company based in St. Louis. I worked on financial data collection and data analysis.

Experiment Game, Oct 2018 - Present

Experiment Game is a android application that I developed for a research project on multi-attribute decision making at the Mind Brain Institute at Hopkins. The project is under the supervision of Dr. Ernst Niebur.

Otoglobal Health, Sep 2019 - Present

In this project, we develop a low-cost otoacoustic emission (OAE) device that easily identifies pediatric hearing loss in developing countries. It originated from a BME design team project and I am part of the software team.

Computational Psycholinguistic Research, Sep 2019 - Dec 2019

This is my independent project on "Assessing LSTM's ability to predict Chinese nominal classifiers based on syntactic cues." I was mentored by Dr. Tal Linzen at the Cognitive Science department of Hopkins.
